Legend of the Five Rings - 10th Century

Custom rules for Legend of the Five Rings RPG using 4th edition rules and set in the 10th century.

Why the 10th Century?

The 10th century is a popular setting for Legend of the Five Rings, as it represents a time of peace before the great upheaval that comes with the Scorpion Clan Coup and the Clan Wars. It is a setting with few legends and heroes, giving the players a blank slate upon which to leave their mark.

Challenges of the 10th Century

The 10th century can be best compared to the Edo period in Japan, where the country was relatively isolated and stable, allowing for a flourishing of culture and society. In the same way, the 10th century in Rokugan is a time of relative peace and stability, with few major conflicts to disrupt the flow of daily life. This thus places a lot of emphasis on the rituals of life and political intrigue in Rokugan versus the lawless nature of the Clan Wars.

Why Custom Rules?

Legend of the Five Rings 4th Edition is no longer a supported system, as such, incorporation of erratas, era-specific rules, and other customizations are necessary to maintain a balanced and enjoyable gaming experience.

Why 4th Edition?

4th Edition provides a solid foundation on which a custom ruleset can be built upon using its' numerous rulebooks released over the many years.

What can I find in this ruleset?

  • Incorporation of official erratas
  • Era specific rules (e.g. Agasha family is still with the Dragon Clan)
  • Fixes to various rules and mechanics
  • Quality of life improvements such as calendars, events and an era specific map
